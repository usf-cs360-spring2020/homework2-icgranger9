Parallel Coordinates Visualization

For this visualization, I used a parallel coordinates plot to show how closely tied parental income median and rank is to the tier school that their child gets in to, and in turn ho closely related that is to their median income and income rank. Unfortunately, the story that this chart tells is in line with what was expected. Parental income plays a clear role in deciding what tier school the child goes to. However, there is some silver lining. For those lower income students (particularly around the 40 thousand mark), we can see that there is a slight clustering of students who went to higher tier schools, and as such had a higher income after graduating.

Data

This data set is composed of information from over 30 million college students, from 1993-2013, and was retrieved from Opportunity Insights. In D3, I was able to filter down to only the information that is relevant to this visualization, which is:

  • Parent Rank: The mean parental income rank,
  • Parent Median: The median parent household income (rounded to nearest $100).
  • Child Median: The median child individual earnings in 2014 (rounded to the nearest $100).
  • Child Rank: The mean child earnings rank.
  • College Tier: For this field, the data provided 14 categories of school, which convey the selectivity and type of college that the child attended. To make distinguishing between these easier, I further grouped them into 7 categories: Elite, Highly Selective, Selective, Non-selective, For Profit, Two Years or Less, and Other. This allowed me to assign a distinct color to each category. The other category represents students that we do not have sufficient data about, as well as those not enrolled in college.
